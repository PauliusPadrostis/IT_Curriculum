# L1–L3 (3) — Data collection + source reliability

**Type:** 📖 Lesson
**Grade:** Grade 11 (III gimnazijos klasė)

**Semester:** Semester 1
**Status:** 🚧 WIP

## Context

**Why here:** everything later depends on trust + traceability (also supports AI ethics later).\
\

## Sub-topics

- Collecting from multiple sources; metadata basics

- Source credibility & bias indicators

- Recording provenance (where data came from, when, how)

## Completion Definition

Student can choose appropriate data sources for a given question and justify reliability by evaluating:

- author/organization credibility

- date/recency

- method/collection context

- bias risk and limitations

Student distinguishes **fact, claim, and interpretation**.

## Minimum Evidence

Student submits:

- a selected dataset/source (link or file)

- a short reliability justification (6–10 sentences) including:

  - 2 credibility indicators

  - 1 limitation

  - 1 bias risk or uncertainty

- a brief note on how the source affects conclusions (1–2 sentences)

## Cognitive Level (Bloom's)

**Bloom:** Analyze → Evaluate

## Readiness Gate

✔ All true:

- reliability justification includes at least 1 concrete limitation

- source choice matches the task (not irrelevant/too narrow)

- distinguishes data from opinion\
  If not → not ready for Workflow 1.
