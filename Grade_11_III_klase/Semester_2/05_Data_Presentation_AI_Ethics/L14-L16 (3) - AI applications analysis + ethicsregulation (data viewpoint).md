# L14–L16 (3) — AI applications analysis + ethics/regulation (data viewpoint)

**Type:** 📖 Lesson
**Grade:** Grade 11 (III gimnazijos klasė)
**Module:** Data – Presentation, AI & Ethics
**Semester:** Semester 2
**Status:** 🚧 WIP

---

## Context

**Grade 11 anchor:** “neuroniniai tinklai… DI etika ir reguliavimas.”\
\

## Sub-topics

- Neural network/AI application interpretation at a user level

- What data drives AI outcomes (training data impact)

- Ethical risks (bias, privacy) + regulation logic (why rules exist)

- Short structured argument writing (2–3 sentences)

## Completion Definition

Student can:

- Explain how AI systems depend on data quality, labeling, bias, and context

- Identify typical failure risks (bias, leakage, spurious correlation, opacity) at a conceptual level

- Apply an ethics/regulation lens:

  - what is permitted / prohibited in a school-life context

  - privacy and fairness considerations

  - accountability (who is responsible for outcomes)

Focus is not “AI hype,” but **data consequences**.

## Minimum Evidence

Student produces:

- a short analysis of one AI use-case (8–12 sentences) including:

  - what data it uses (and what can go wrong)

  - one concrete bias/fairness risk

  - one privacy risk (if applicable)

  - one mitigation idea (data/process change)

- plus a 3–5 bullet “regulation/ethics checklist” for deploying such a system responsibly

## Cognitive Level (Bloom's)

**Bloom:** Understand → Evaluate

## Readiness Gate

✔ All true:

- Identifies at least one valid risk tied to data (not vague “AI is bad”)

- Mentions at least one mitigation that is practical

- Uses ethics/regulation language correctly (privacy/fairness/accountability)\
  If not → not ready for argument-response prep (P3) and A4.
