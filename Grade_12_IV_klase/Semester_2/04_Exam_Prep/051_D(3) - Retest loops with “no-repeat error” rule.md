# D51 (3) — Retest loops with “no-repeat error” rule

**Type:** 🔁 Diagnostic
**Grade:** Grade 12 (IV gimnazijos klasė)
**Module:** Exam Preparation – VBE Simulation
**Semester:** Semester 2
**Sequence:** 51 of grade year
**Lessons allocated:** 3
**Status:** 🚧 WIP

---

## Context

- Students redo comparable tasks with constraint: they must demonstrate that the previous error class is eliminated
- Marking emphasizes *error absence* and *process reliability*

## Completion Definition

Student independently completes structured retest tasks under the explicit rule:

> Previously identified major error categories must not reappear.

Student demonstrates:

- Corrected execution in areas previously unstable

- Stable performance under timed conditions

- Explicit verification before submission

- Zero recurrence of documented error types

This sub-topic confirms error elimination, not just temporary correction.

---

## Minimum Evidence

For each retest loop, student submits:

- Completed timed task (Data, Programming, or mixed depending on weakness)

- Error log from prior mock (referenced)

- Explicit statement:

  - “Previously observed error type: \__\_”

  - “Preventive strategy applied: \__\_”

- Final output demonstrating no recurrence of the targeted error

No scaffolding. No guided correction.

---

## Cognitive Level (Bloom's)

**Bloom: Analyze → Apply → Evaluate**

- Analyze prior failure pattern

- Apply corrected strategy deliberately

- Evaluate performance against “no-repeat” constraint

This is deliberate stabilization training.

---

## Readiness Gate

✔ Previously documented major error does not reappear\
✔ Task completed within time threshold\
✔ Output formatting exact\
✔ No runtime or structural failures\
✔ Verification step visibly performed (student self-check log)

If the same major error category reappears → Gate not met.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Fixing symptom but not mechanism → recurrence

- Forgetting preventive strategy under time pressure

- Regression in formatting precision

- Time collapse during retest

- Overconfidence leading to skipped verification
