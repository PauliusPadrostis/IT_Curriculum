# I9 (2) — Workflow 3: Compare Two Report Methods (Subtotals vs Pivot)

**Type:** 🔗 Integration
**Grade:** Grade 12 (IV gimnazijos klasė)
**Module:** Data – Reporting & Analysis
**Semester:** Semester 1
**Sequence:** 9 of grade year
**Lessons allocated:** 2
**Status:** 🚧 WIP

---

## Context

**Integration target**

- Same question solved two ways; students justify which is clearer and why.

## Completion Definition

Student independently produces **two reports from the same dataset**:

- One using **Grouping + Subtotals**

- One using a **Pivot Table**

Student then provides a structured comparison that:

- Identifies at least **two structural differences**

- Identifies at least **one efficiency or flexibility difference**

- States which method is more appropriate for a specified reporting scenario and justifies the choice

Comparison must reference concrete structural characteristics, not vague preferences.

---

## Minimum Evidence

Student submits:

- Subtotal-based report

- Pivot-based report

- A written comparison (5–8 sentences) including:

  - ≥2 structural differences

  - ≥1 functional/efficiency difference

  - A justified method selection for a given scenario

No prompts. No comparison template.

---

## Cognitive Level (Bloom's)

**Bloom: Analyze → Evaluate**

- Analyze structural mechanics of both methods

- Evaluate suitability under scenario constraints

- Justify decision using technical reasoning

Within Grade 12 VBE 2 expectations (method reasoning, not invention).

---

## Readiness Gate

✔ All must be true:

- Both reports are technically correct and internally consistent

- Differences identified are structurally valid (not superficial formatting)

- Chosen method aligns logically with scenario requirement

- Justification contains explicit reference to method capability (e.g., refreshability, dynamic dimensions, grouping rigidity)

If comparison is preference-based or structurally incorrect → Gate not met.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Comparing formatting instead of mechanics

- Claiming pivot is “better” without specifying why

- Not recognizing that subtotals require pre-sorting

- Failing to understand pivot dimensional flexibility

- Producing only one correct report (comparison invalid)

- Writing generic evaluation without scenario grounding
