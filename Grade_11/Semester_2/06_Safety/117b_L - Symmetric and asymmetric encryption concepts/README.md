# L — Symmetric and asymmetric encryption: concepts and demonstration

**Type:** 📖 Lesson
**Grade:** Grade 11
**Module:** Safety – Advanced Topics
**Semester:** Semester 2
**Status:** 🚧 WIP
**Note:** This is an IN-CLASS lesson, not theory-loaded. Crypto requires hands-on demonstration.

---

## Overview

- Symmetric encryption: same key encrypts and decrypts (AES example)
- Asymmetric encryption: public key encrypts, private key decrypts (RSA concept)
- Why asymmetric solves the key distribution problem
- Live demonstration: encrypting/decrypting a message using an online tool or GPG4Win/Kleopatra
- Certificates: what they are, why browsers trust them, what "padlock" means

## Sub-topics

- Symmetric vs asymmetric encryption comparison
- Public and private key concept
- Certificate and certificate authority concept
- Practical demonstration with encryption tool

---

## 1️⃣ Completion Definition

Student can explain the difference between symmetric and asymmetric encryption, describe the role of certificates, and has seen encryption/decryption performed.

## 2️⃣ Minimum Evidence

Correctly answers: "Why can't symmetric encryption alone secure internet communication?" and "What does a certificate prove?"

## 3️⃣ Bloom's Taxonomy Level

Level 2 — Understand

## 4️⃣ Readiness Gate

None (standalone topic).

## 5️⃣ Common Failure Modes

- Confusing public and private keys (which encrypts, which decrypts)
- Thinking encryption = unbreakable
- Not understanding why certificates matter
