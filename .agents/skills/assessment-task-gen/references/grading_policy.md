## Rules

1. **Difficulty distribution** (§29.3): 20% easy, 50% medium, 30% hard. Items progress from easier to harder within one assessment.

2. **Student notification** (§29.2): Inform students at least 1 week before the assessment. Share: structure, content scope, goals, and evaluation criteria.

3. **Points required** (§29.6): Every question and task must show its point value. No hidden scoring.

4. **Assessment types and durations:**

   | Type | Duration | Use Case |
   |------|----------|----------|
   | Atsiskaitomasis darbas | ≥30 min | Major summative assessment |
   | Apklausa raštu | 15-20 min | Short written quiz |
   | Savarankiškas darbas | 10-20 min | Independent work check |

5. **Item ordering** (§29.3): Questions must progress from easier to harder.

6. **Academic integrity** (§29.9.5): Teacher may void work if copying is suspected. May reassess the student orally.

7. **Results timeline** (§29.7): Graded and returned to students within 2 weeks. Results analyzed with the class. Individual failures discussed privately.

8. **Assessment limit** (§30): Maximum 1 graded assessment per day for grades I-II (gymnasium).

9. **Rubric sharing**: Evaluation criteria (rubric) must be shared with students before the assessment (per §29.2).
